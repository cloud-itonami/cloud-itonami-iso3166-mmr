# Operator guide — MMR

Portal: Myanmar Companies Online (MyCO, `myco.dica.gov.mm`), the
Directorate of Investment and Company Administration's (DICA) own
official company registry, operated under the Ministry of Investment and
Foreign Economic Relations (MIFER). An investor registers a company
under the Myanmar Companies Law 2017 / Myanmar Companies Regulations
2018 via MyCO, then applies for an investment permit or endorsement from
the Myanmar Investment Commission (MIC), administered by DICA -- DICA's
own text states a proposal "will be accepted within 15 days" if it meets
criteria, then MIC "will review the proposal and reach a decision within
60 days" (no capital threshold distinguishing when a full permit, as
opposed to just an endorsement, is required is stated on any DICA page
reachable this session -- an honest gap).

Ongoing compliance requires filing an Annual Return (AR) each year by a
company-specific due date shown in the company's own MyCO profile. Per
DICA's own guidance (own text, `dica.gov.mm/en/annual-return`): a 50,000
MMK filing fee is due by that date; a 100,000 MMK late fee applies if
filed within 28 days after the due date; if more than 28 days pass
unfiled, the company's status becomes "Suspended" (lifting the resulting
Form I-9D suspension flag costs an additional 100,000 MMK); after 6
further months of continued non-filing while Suspended, the company is
"Struck Off" the register (reinstatement requires a court clearance
order plus Form I-5). Filings that claim an Annual Return status
("current" / "late-fee-due") that does not match the independently
recomputed status for the company's own declared due date and filed date
clear this staged-deadline gate before a filing can proceed. Human-gated
filing only.
